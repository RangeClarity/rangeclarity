# RangeClarity Content Strategy

## Homepage Positioning

RangeClarity is a premium TradingView indicator suite for reading range, momentum, entry quality, and risk before making the move.

Primary positioning:

> Premium TradingView indicators for reading the range before you make the move.

RangeClarity should be presented as an overlay-first indicator pack, not a generic investor dashboard. The landing page should make the product category obvious in the first viewport.

## Homepage Structure

Use this order for the primary landing page:

1. Hero: TradingView indicator suite headline, early-access CTA, module CTA, and no-hype trust line.
2. Indicator modules: Range Map Overlay, Momentum Engine, and Risk Radar as premium TradingView indicator cards.
3. How it works: install on TradingView, add indicators, read range, confirm momentum, decide buy / wait / avoid.
4. Mock TradingView chart: CSS/SVG-only ASTS preview with support, resistance, current price, range score, momentum, entry quality, and risk zone.
5. Who it is for: long-term investors, swing traders, retail chart readers, watchlist builders, and indicator minimalists.
6. What RangeClarity is not: signal bot, financial advice, guaranteed profit machine, indicator spaghetti, or gambling tool.
7. Pricing / early access: placeholder only, with future Free Preview, Pro Indicator Suite, and AI Companion Add-on.
8. FAQ and trust notes: answer TradingView, signals, repaint, access, mock data, and target user questions.
9. Final CTA: `Stop chasing candles. Start reading the range.`

## Reference Research Summary

Read `RANGECLARITY_REFERENCE_RESEARCH.md` before future conversion or visual hierarchy passes.

Adapted patterns:

- Official indicator sites use named product bundles, pricing cards, setup flows, testimonials/FAQ, and risk disclaimers.
- Dark fintech galleries emphasize high-contrast cards, compact stats, neon accents, and strong above-the-fold product previews.
- Fintech landing pages convert best when the value proposition, proof, CTA, trust notes, and FAQ are easy to scan.

Do not copy competitor claims, text, mascots, layouts, screenshots, logos, or pricing.

## Indicator Module Copy

### Range Map Overlay

Purpose:

Shows where price sits inside the active range.

Core bullets:

- Auto-detected support and resistance zones
- Price position inside the current range
- Range score from 0-100
- Wait / Watch / Act context
- Helps avoid chasing near resistance

Best for:

Long-term investors checking whether a stock is early, fair, or late inside the range.

### Momentum Engine

Purpose:

Shows whether the move is strengthening, fading, compressing, or breaking out.

Core bullets:

- Momentum stage detection
- Trend strength
- Breakout watch
- Rejection / exhaustion context
- Useful for timing entries better

Best for:

Swing traders and watchlist builders who want momentum context before entering.

### Risk Radar

Purpose:

Shows whether the current entry has attractive upside versus nearby downside.

Core bullets:

- Risk/reward context
- Late-entry warnings
- Pullback zones
- Downside mapping
- Entry quality label

Best for:

Retail investors who want cleaner buy-zone timing and fewer late entries.

## FAQ Ideas

Possible FAQ questions:

- Is RangeClarity a TradingView indicator?
- Does RangeClarity repaint?
- Is this financial advice?
- Does RangeClarity provide buy and sell signals?
- Which markets will it support?
- Will there be a free preview?
- How will early access work?
- Will the AI Chart Companion make trades or recommendations?
- Can I use it for long-term investing and swing trading?

Suggested FAQ tone:

Clear, calm, direct. Avoid hype. Give honest prototype-stage answers.

Useful short answers:

- RangeClarity is being positioned as a TradingView indicator suite, not a brokerage app.
- No-repaint language should stay conservative until the real indicator implementation proves it.
- RangeClarity can help frame buy / wait / avoid context, but it should not issue guaranteed signals.
- The prototype uses mock data and does not connect to live market feeds.

## Future AI Companion Positioning

AI should be positioned as an optional explanation layer, not the core product.

Good framing:

- Useful for learning what the modules are showing

Avoid:

- AI tells you what to buy
- AI replaces risk management
