## 1. Hero Patterns

Best patterns to adapt:

- Lead with the exact product category in the first viewport.
- Pair a short, memorable headline with a visible product preview or proof strip.
- Use a small credibility row before or near the headline.
- Put the primary CTA above the fold and repeat it at natural decision points.
- Show platform fit early: TradingView-first, overlay-first, mock preview only.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Keep the current hero headline.
- Add compact proof cards under the CTAs: suite packaging, decision posture, launch posture.
- Keep the "No repaint hype. No guaranteed signals." line because it is honest and brand-fit.

## 2. Pricing Patterns

Best patterns to adapt:

- Plans are easy to scan when they include a title, status, one-line purpose, and included bullets.
- Pricing sections can also include setup expectations and cancellation/refund answers.
- Guarantees and trial claims must be avoided unless they are real.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Keep pricing as "coming soon."
- Present three future shapes: Free Preview, Pro Indicator Suite, AI Companion Add-on.
- Add bullets that explain likely contents without implying checkout exists.
- Add explicit notes that no payments, account flows, or access management are built.

## 3. Indicator Module Packaging

Best patterns to adapt:

- Named modules make complex indicator products feel more tangible.
- Each module needs purpose, visual output, stat language, and what it helps avoid.
- Packages feel more premium when the page explains what appears on the chart.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Preserve Range Map Overlay, Momentum Engine, and Risk Radar.
- Add "Included layer" and "Chart output" copy to each module card.
- Keep mock visuals CSS/SVG only.

## 4. Trust, FAQ, And Disclaimer Ideas

Best patterns to adapt:

- Strong competitor pages answer platform, access, markets, beginner fit, refunds, and win-rate questions.
- Trading product pages need prominent risk language near pricing and FAQs.
- Avoid "win every trade" framing except to clearly reject it.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Add FAQ section after pricing.
- Include clear answers about TradingView, signals, repaint claims, access model, mock data, and target user.
- Add a concise disclaimer that RangeClarity is educational chart context, not advice or guaranteed results.

## 5. Dark Card Visual Ideas

Best patterns to adapt:

- Dark UI works when cards use strong contrast, neon accent edges, compact metadata, and small visual surfaces.
- Dense data cards should still have breathing room and stable dimensions.
- Multiple accent colors keep the page from becoming one-note.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Keep mint, signal blue, and amber as module-specific accents.
- Use small proof cards and included-layer cards instead of large generic feature cards.
- Keep all cards at 8px radius and market-toolkit density.

## 6. TradingView Setup Flow Ideas

Best patterns to adapt:

- A simple setup flow lowers friction: join, provide TradingView username or access details, activate, add indicators, read chart.
- Prototype pages should not claim automated setup when it is not built.
- Setup guides and example layouts are conversion assets.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Keep the five-step flow: install, add indicators, read range, confirm momentum, decide buy / wait / avoid.
- Add setup highlights: TradingView home base, access model TBD, setup material planned.
- Avoid live integration claims.

## 7. Conversion Ideas

Best patterns to adapt:

- Repeated CTAs after proof-heavy sections.
- FAQ near pricing to reduce uncertainty.
- Make the offer concrete even before pricing is final.
- Treat disclaimers as trust signals, not footnote sludge.

RangeClarity adaptation:

- Strengthen early-access cards.
- Add FAQ before final CTA.
- Repeat "Join Early Access" after pricing and in final CTA.
- Keep the final close as "Stop chasing candles. Start reading the range."
