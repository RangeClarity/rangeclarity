# TradingView Onboarding Guide

## Purpose

This guide explains how a paid beta user gets set up with RangeClarity inside TradingView. It is written for manual beta access and should be updated before public launch.

## Create or Open a TradingView Account

1. Open TradingView.
2. Create an account or sign in to your existing account.
3. Confirm your profile username.
4. Keep the username available for the beta access form.

## Where to Enter Your TradingView Username

After payment/test payment, you will be asked for your TradingView username. Enter the exact username from your TradingView profile.

Do not enter your email address unless your email is also your visible TradingView username.

## How Invite-Only Indicator Access Works

RangeClarity beta access is granted manually from TradingView. After your payment/test payment and username are verified, the founder/admin adds your TradingView username to the invite-only script access list.

During beta, this is not instant. Manual approval helps catch spelling issues and account mismatches.

## What to Expect After Payment

1. You complete payment/test payment.
2. You submit your TradingView username.
3. Your access enters manual review.
4. You receive an access granted email when the invite is ready.
5. You open TradingView and add the indicator from invite-only scripts.

## Add RangeClarity to a Chart

1. Open TradingView.
2. Open a daily or weekly chart.
3. Open Indicators.
4. Find invite-only scripts.
5. Select RangeClarity.
6. Add it to the chart.
7. Confirm the dashboard and zones appear.

## Save Your Layout

1. Add RangeClarity to your preferred chart.
2. Set the timeframe you want to review.
3. Keep the chart clean.
4. Save the layout in TradingView.

Start with daily or weekly charts for beta testing. Lower timeframes can be noisier and are not the first validation focus.

## Use RangeClarity Responsibly

RangeClarity is a chart-structure clarity tool. Use it to understand:

- Market setup
- Support and resistance zones
- Distance to key areas
- Zone strength
- Whether structure is clean or unclear

It is not a replacement for your process, risk rules, or independent judgment.

## What RangeClarity Does Not Do

RangeClarity does not:

- Provide financial advice
- Predict price
- Guarantee outcomes
- Provide trade instructions
- Replace TradingView or your own review process
- Manage risk for you

## Troubleshooting

### I cannot see RangeClarity in TradingView

Check that:

- You are signed into the correct TradingView account.
- You submitted the correct username.
- You received the access granted email.
- You are looking under invite-only scripts.

### I submitted the wrong username

Reply to the access email with the correct TradingView username.

### The indicator appears but looks different from screenshots

Check:

- Timeframe
- Symbol
- Indicator settings
- Whether enough chart history is loaded
