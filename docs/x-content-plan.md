# 14-Day X Content Plan

## Goal

Use X to build trust before the paid/private beta. The content should make RangeClarity feel calm, premium, and useful without drifting into hype or trade calls.

Default beta offer: Founding Beta Access, $79 for 90 days.

## Core Positioning Lines

- Simple chart. Complex engine.
- No signals. No noise. Just structure.
- Clarity over noise.

## Content Rules

- Describe structure, do not direct action.
- No predictions.
- No performance promises.
- No trade calls.
- No screenshots implying guaranteed outcomes.
- Show "unclear" or "no clean structure" examples often.
- Keep posts short, specific, and repeatable.

## Posting Cadence

- 1 primary post per day.
- 1 optional reply/thread expansion per day.
- 1 chart/context example every 2-3 days.
- 1 beta access CTA every 3-4 days, not every post.

## Day 1

**Theme:** Positioning

**Post idea:** RangeClarity is not another noisy indicator stack. It is a clean structure read for traders who want to understand the chart before making their own decision.

**CTA:** Follow for beta notes.

## Day 2

**Theme:** Simple chart. Complex engine.

**Post idea:** The surface should be simple: trend, range, support, resistance, distance, and structure. The engine can be complex. The chart should not be.

**CTA:** None.

## Day 3

**Theme:** No signals. No noise. Just structure.

**Post idea:** A good tool should be allowed to say "unclear." If every chart looks urgent, the tool is part of the problem.

**CTA:** Beta waitlist mention.

## Day 4

**Theme:** Support/resistance clarity

**Post idea:** A support/resistance map should not show every possible level. It should surface the few zones that matter most right now.

**CTA:** None.

## Day 5

**Theme:** Range location

**Post idea:** The same chart can look completely different near support, in the middle of a range, or near resistance. Location changes the read.

**CTA:** Ask: "What do you check first: trend, level, volume, or range?"
